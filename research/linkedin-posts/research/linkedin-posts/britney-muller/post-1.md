# Post by Britney Muller
**Date:** 2025 (BrightonSEO keynote period; cross-posted to LinkedIn)
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/britneymuller_ai-the-double-edged-sword-seizing-opportunities-activity-7130254109722451968-HFxB

**Summary (paraphrased):**
Following her BrightonSEO keynote "AI's Double-Edged Sword," Britney Muller shared a core framing that has stuck with her audience: AI is not a "truth engine" — it's an "average-generating machine." Her point is that how positively (or negatively) a brand or person shows up across many relevant pages and search results shapes how AI systems characterize them, essentially by averaging the sentiment and framing found across the web. She paired this with warnings about the ethical dimensions of AI systems often overlooked in SEO/marketing conversations — including how training datasets for major models can contain deeply flawed or harmful material, which she argues marketers should understand rather than treat AI outputs as neutral or objective.

---
**Why this post matters:** Muller's "average-generating machine" framing gives a concrete mental model for why sentiment and consistency across a brand's web presence affects AI-generated summaries — a genuinely useful, technically grounded idea for anyone building a content strategy aimed at AI visibility, distinct from more tactical "how to get cited" advice.
