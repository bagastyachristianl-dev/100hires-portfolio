**Date:** February 2026 (approximate, based on activity ID)
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/thinkingslow_in-the-last-3-months-at-ahrefs-we-analyzed-activity-7424382429542486016--OIQ

**Summary (paraphrased):**
Ryan Law shared findings from an Ahrefs study analyzing over 1 billion data points across 11 separate research projects on AI search optimization. Key findings he highlighted: YouTube mentions are the single strongest predictor of AI visibility, outperforming Domain Rating, backlinks, or any traditional SEO ranking factor. AI Mode and AI Overviews reach the same conclusions for a given query about 86% of the time, but cite almost entirely different sources — only about 14% citation overlap. Content length has essentially no correlation with AI citation likelihood, with the majority of AI Overview citations going to pages under 1,000 words. He also noted Google still sends far more traffic than ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity combined, even though ChatGPT accounts for the large majority of AI-driven referral traffic specifically.

---
**Why this post matters:** This is a rare example of large-scale, original quantitative research (1B+ data points, 11 studies) on what actually correlates with AI search visibility, directly contradicting common assumptions (e.g. that long-form content or high Domain Rating drives AI citations). It's exactly the kind of primary data a real AI SEO playbook should be built on.
