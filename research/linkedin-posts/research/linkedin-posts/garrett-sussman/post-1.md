# Post by Garrett Sussman
**Date:** May 29, 2026 (approximate, based on related iPullRank publication date)
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/garrettsussman/ (shared summary of iPullRank's "Beyond Rankings" AI search metrics research)

**Summary (paraphrased):**
Garrett Sussman shared iPullRank's framework for measuring AI search performance now that traditional rank tracking no longer captures what's actually happening. He argued that the foundational assumptions behind SEO measurement are breaking down — more pages no longer means more traffic, and a single keyword now fans out into many different AI-generated query variations, each producing a different result. His proposed framework splits measurement into three layers: Input Metrics (is the content mathematically relevant to user intent, how fresh is it), Channel Metrics (is the brand being recommended or cited by AI engines, is it seen as a trusted source for a given entity), and Performance Metrics (the traditional bottom line — traffic, conversions, revenue). He was explicit that this measurement approach differs by industry and site type, and that this is one of the first structured attempts to translate SEO reporting into a language AI-driven search still supports.

---
**Why this post matters:** This reflects direct output from iPullRank's Relevance Engineering research team, translating a genuinely novel measurement problem (attribution in a world without fixed rankings) into a practical, three-layer framework — the kind of structural thinking a real AI SEO playbook needs, not just tactics.
