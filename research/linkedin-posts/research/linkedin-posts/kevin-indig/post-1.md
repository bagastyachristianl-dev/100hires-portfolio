# Post by Kevin Indig
**Date:** 2026 (referenced in Position Digital's AI SEO statistics roundup, April 2026)
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_ai-overviews-vs-organic-links-activity-7398354007754244096-ZYix

**Summary (paraphrased):**
Kevin Indig published original research through his Growth Memo newsletter (also shared on LinkedIn) finding that a significant share of ChatGPT citations — roughly 44% — come from the first third of a piece of content, and that ChatGPT favors content with direct definitions, a balanced/neutral tone, and dense entity mentions. The practical implication he draws: front-loading the most important information, avoiding an overly promotional or biased tone, and packing early paragraphs with named entities (people, tools, companies, concepts) measurably improves the likelihood of being cited by ChatGPT specifically, rather than relying on cited authority alone. He has also published separate, contrasting research noting that despite earlier growth, AI referral traffic (from ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, etc.) declined significantly in late 2025 as model changes altered how models generate answers and link out.

---
**Why this post matters:** Kevin Indig's advisory work spans multiple large tech companies, and this specific finding (citation concentration in the first third of content) is a concrete, testable content-structure recommendation backed by original data — exactly the kind of granular, evidence-based tactic a real AI SEO playbook needs, as opposed to generic "write good content" advice.
