# Post by Aleyda Solís
**Date:** June 8, 2026
**URL:** https://www.aleydasolis.com/en/ai-search/ai-search-prompt-library/ (shared and summarized on LinkedIn the same day)

**Summary (paraphrased):**
Aleyda Solís published a detailed framework for building a "representative" AI search prompt library, addressing what she considers one of the most common mistakes in how teams measure AI search visibility: using an unrepresentative or tool-default set of prompts. Her argument is that if the prompt set doesn't reflect how real customers actually phrase their questions, the resulting visibility data is inaccurate from the start — no amount of downstream analysis fixes a flawed input layer. She recommends starting with 30-50 prompts built from actual business questions and audience language (not assumptions), organized by topic and customer journey stage, and mapping any visibility gaps to one of three causes: a content gap, a source credibility issue, or an entity recognition problem.

---
**Why this post matters:** Most AI SEO commentary focuses on output — what content ranks or gets cited. Aleyda's post addresses a step most people skip entirely: whether the *inputs* used to measure AI visibility are even valid. It's a methodologically rigorous, practitioner-level correction that would directly shape how a real AI SEO playbook should structure its measurement process.
