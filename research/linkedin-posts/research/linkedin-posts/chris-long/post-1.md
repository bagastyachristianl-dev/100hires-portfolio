# Post by Chris Long
**Date:** April 2026 (approximate)
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/chris-long-marketing_whoa-this-seo-article-shows-that-chatgpt-activity-7437844820280193024-fTrn (and related posts on GPT-5.4 fan-out behavior, cited in Position Digital's AI SEO statistics roundup)

**Summary (paraphrased):**
Chris Long shared original findings on how ChatGPT's newer model (GPT-5.4) changed its search behavior compared to earlier versions: it now runs significantly more fan-out queries per prompt (10+ variations) and increasingly uses "site:" search operators to pull information directly from a brand's own domain rather than third-party sources that mention the brand. He illustrated this with concrete examples — for a query like "best nursing programs," the fan-out sub-queries included specific technical terms like "NCLEX pass rates" and "CCNE accreditation"; for "best SEO agency," the sub-queries looked for industry award wins. His broader point: ChatGPT is increasingly looking for concrete authority signals embedded in a page's actual content, not just brand mentions elsewhere on the web.

---
**Why this post matters:** This is granular, mechanism-level research on how a specific, current LLM (GPT-5.4) actually decomposes and executes search queries — the kind of first-hand technical observation that can't be found in generic "AI SEO tips" content, and directly informs what content structure would actually get surfaced.
